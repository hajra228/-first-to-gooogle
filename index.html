What is a hacker?

A hacker is someone who explores technologies creatively. Historically the word has covered tinkerers, software explorers, security researchers, and people who break systems. Today we distinguish by intent and legality:

Curious
Problem-solver
Security-minded

Short definition

A hacker is a person who applies deep technical skill to understand, modify, or break systems. That can be used for good (security research, improving defenses) or for harm (illegal intrusion).

Quick facts
  • Hacking ≠ Crime (intent matters)
  • Ethical hacking is often called "white hat"
  • Practice legally via CTFs & labs

Types of hackers (by intent)

White Hat

Security professionals who test systems with permission to find and fix vulnerabilities.

Grey Hat

May explore vulnerabilities without clear permission; sometimes disclose responsibly, sometimes not. Risky legally.

Black Hat

Individuals who break systems or exploit vulnerabilities for personal gain or harm. Illegal and unethical.

Hacktivist

Use hacking to advance political or social causes — can cross legal lines.

Script Kiddie

Inexperienced users who use pre-built tools without deep understanding — often harmful.

How to learn (the ethical path)

If you want to learn cybersecurity and "hacking" without breaking the law, follow a safe roadmap:

  1. Basics: Learn operating systems (Linux), networking (TCP/IP), and programming (Python).
  2. Security fundamentals: Understand web security (HTTP, cookies), cryptography basics, and system hardening.
  3. Hands-on labs: Use legal CTF platforms and intentionally vulnerable VMs.
  4. Responsible disclosure: If you find vulnerabilities, follow coordinated disclosure policies (report to vendor or CERT).
  5. Certifications: Consider CEH, OSCP, CompTIA Security+, or vendor trainings for professional development.
I will not provide instructions for illegal intrusion, nor links to tools intended to break into systems. Use your skills for defense, research, and learning on authorized platforms only.

Careers and how to break in

Roles

  • Penetration Tester
  • Security Engineer
  • Red Team Specialist
  • Blue Team / Incident Responder
  • Security Researcher

Steps to get started

  1. Build a portfolio: write-ups, CTF solves, GitHub projects.
  2. Gain certifications: OSCP, CEH, Security+.
  3. Join local security groups, attend conferences.

Get started — free learning plan (30 days)

  1. Days 1–7: Linux basics, shell, networking fundamentals
  2. Days 8–14: Python scripting, HTTP basics, browsers & devtools
  3. Days 15–21: Web security fundamentals (XSS, SQLi), OWASP Top 10
  4. Days 22–30: Join TryHackMe & solve beginner rooms; write 2-3 writeups

This plan focuses on building defensive knowledge and responsible testing skills.
